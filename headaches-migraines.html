Understanding Headaches and Migraines

There are several types of headaches, each with different causes and triggers:

  • Tension Headaches – Often caused by muscle tension in the neck and shoulders, poor posture, or stress
  • Cervicogenic Headaches – Originate from dysfunction in the cervical spine (neck), causing referred pain to the head
  • Migraines – Neurological condition causing intense, throbbing pain, often accompanied by nausea, light sensitivity, and visual disturbances
  • Cluster Headaches – Severe headaches that occur in cyclical patterns or clusters

Many headaches and migraines have a mechanical or neurological component — meaning they can be triggered or worsened by issues in the spine, nervous system, or muscular imbalances.
