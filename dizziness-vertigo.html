Understanding Dizziness and Vertigo

Dizziness and vertigo are not the same, though they're often used interchangeably:

  • Dizziness – A general term for feeling lightheaded, unsteady, or off-balance
  • Vertigo – A specific type of dizziness where you feel like you or your surroundings are spinning or moving

Common Causes of Dizziness and Vertigo

  • Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo (BPPV) – Displaced crystals in the inner ear causing brief episodes of vertigo with head movement
  • Cervicogenic Dizziness – Dizziness originating from dysfunction in the cervical spine (neck)
  • Vestibular Neuritis – Inflammation of the vestibular nerve affecting balance
  • Meniere's Disease – Inner ear disorder causing vertigo, tinnitus, and hearing loss
  • Concussion or Mild Traumatic Brain Injury – Neurological symptoms following head trauma
  • Vertebrobasilar Insufficiency – Reduced blood flow to the brainstem and inner ear
  • Medication Side Effects – Certain medications can cause dizziness as a side effect

How We Treat Dizziness and Vertigo

Comprehensive Neurological and Vestibular Evaluation

We perform a thorough examination to identify the specific cause of your dizziness or vertigo, including tests for BPPV, cervical dysfunction, and neurological issues.

Vestibular Rehabilitation Therapy

We provide targeted exercises designed to retrain your balance system, reduce dizziness, and improve stability. These exercises are customized to your specific condition.

Epley Maneuver and Canalith Repositioning

For BPPV, we perform specialized maneuvers to reposition displaced crystals in the inner ear, often providing immediate relief.

Cervical Spine Adjustments

If your dizziness is related to cervical dysfunction, gentle adjustments can restore proper alignment and reduce nerve irritation, alleviating symptoms.

Neurological Rehabilitation

We use specific exercises and therapies to improve neurological function, particularly in cases involving concussion or vestibular neuritis.

Lifestyle and Dietary Guidance

For conditions like Meniere's disease, we provide guidance on dietary modifications and lifestyle changes to reduce symptom frequency and severity.
