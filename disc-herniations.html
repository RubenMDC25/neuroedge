Non-Surgical Treatment for Disc Bulges and Herniations in Murrieta, CA

Disc bulges and herniations are among the most common causes of chronic back pain and sciatica. At NeuroEdge Chiropractic, we offer advanced, non-surgical treatments that address the root cause of your disc-related pain, helping you avoid surgery and get back to living pain-free.

Understanding Disc Bulges and Herniations

Your spinal discs act as cushions between the vertebrae, absorbing shock and allowing for flexibility. When a disc is damaged, it can:

  • Bulge – The outer layer of the disc weakens, causing it to protrude beyond its normal boundary
  • Herniate – The inner gel-like material breaks through the outer layer, potentially compressing nearby nerves

Both conditions can cause:

  • Localized back or neck pain
  • Radiating pain down the arms or legs (sciatica/radiculopathy)
  • Numbness, tingling, or weakness
  • Loss of mobility and function

Our Treatment Approach for Disc Problems

At NeuroEdge Chiropractic, we use a comprehensive, non-invasive approach to treat disc bulges and herniations:

Spinal Decompression Therapy

Our state-of-the-art decompression table gently stretches the spine, creating negative pressure within the disc. This helps retract herniated or bulging material, reduces nerve compression, and promotes healing by improving nutrient flow to the damaged disc.

Chiropractic Adjustments

Precise spinal adjustments restore proper alignment and reduce mechanical stress on the affected disc, supporting long-term healing and preventing future issues.

SoftWave Therapy

Advanced acoustic wave therapy stimulates natural healing processes, reduces inflammation around the affected disc, and accelerates tissue repair.

Targeted Rehabilitation

We provide customized exercises to strengthen core muscles, improve spinal stability, and support proper posture — all essential for long-term disc health.

Postural and Ergonomic Coaching

We help you identify and correct daily habits that may be contributing to disc stress, including sitting posture, lifting techniques, and work ergonomics.
