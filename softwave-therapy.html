What to Expect from SoftWave® Therapy in Murrieta

What it is (in plain English):

SoftWave Therapy uses focused acoustic (sound) waves delivered through a handheld device to “wake up” your body's natural repair system. Those waves increase local blood flow, calm inflammation, and signal repair cells (including resident stem/repair cells) to get to work—so irritated tissues can recover faster without injections or downtime.

How it works:

The applicator glides over the skin with a small amount of gel while it sends controlled pulses into the tissue below. These pulses create a brief pressure change that triggers a healing cascade: circulation improves, inflammatory mediators down-shift, and the area begins remodeling rather than staying stuck in a painful, inflamed state.

Our process at NeuroEdge Chiropractic:

When you choose NeuroEdge Chiropractic for SoftWave Therapy, you'll get a personalized, attentive visit led by Dr. Carlson and our clinical team. We start with a thorough evaluation of your condition and goals, then design a plan tailored to your needs—often paired with targeted rehab or chiropractic adjustments so results last.

What you'll feel during treatment:

Most patients describe firm “tapping” pulses. Sessions typically last 10–15 minutes. Discomfort is minimal and adjustable; there's no downtime, so you can return to normal activities right away.

Typical timeline:

  • Acute issues (new injuries) may improve quickly.
  • Chronic problems usually respond over a short series—about 4–8 visits, spaced as clinically appropriate.

After your session:

Mild soreness for 24–48 hours is normal as tissues respond. We'll give you simple do's and don'ts and, when helpful, exercises to support healing between visits.

Understanding How SoftWave Therapy Works

SoftWave Therapy—also known as SoftWave Tissue Regeneration Technology—uses patented acoustic wave technology to stimulate the body's natural healing processes. The device delivers high-energy sound waves to targeted areas, reaching deep into muscles, tendons, and ligaments without damaging surrounding tissues.

When these waves interact with the affected area, they:

  • Increase blood flow to enhance oxygen and nutrient delivery
  • Activate dormant stem cells to promote tissue regeneration
  • Reduce inflammation and swelling
  • Break down scar tissue to improve flexibility

This combination helps athletes recover from acute and chronic injuries more effectively, reducing the need for downtime.

The Role of SoftWave Pain Relief in Sports Recovery

Athletic injuries often cause inflammation and microtears in muscles or ligaments that lead to persistent pain. While rest and traditional therapies can help, they often take weeks or months for full recovery. SoftWave pain relief provides a faster solution by accelerating the healing cycle from the inside out.

This therapy is especially effective for:

  • Muscle strains and sprains
  • Tendonitis or overuse injuries
  • Plantar fasciitis and shin splints
  • Shoulder, knee, or elbow pain
  • Chronic inflammation or scar tissue buildup

By addressing the root cause rather than masking symptoms, SoftWave Therapy enables long-term improvement. Many athletes report reduced pain and improved mobility after just a few sessions—making it a trusted option for those who want to return to training without compromising their recovery.

What to Expect During a SoftWave Therapy Session

A typical session begins with a brief evaluation of the affected area to determine the optimal treatment settings. The SoftWave device is then applied to the skin, emitting gentle acoustic pulses. Most patients describe the sensation as mild discomfort or pressure, but it's generally well-tolerated.

Sessions usually last 10–15 minutes, depending on the size of the treatment area. Many patients experience noticeable relief after the first few sessions, though a complete treatment plan may involve multiple visits for lasting results.

Athletes often combine SoftWave Therapy with stretching, strengthening exercises, and chiropractic care to maximize recovery outcomes.
